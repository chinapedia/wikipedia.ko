On Wikipedia, [talk pages](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:TP "wikilink") serve a variety of functions. One of the main purposes is for Wikipedia editors to discuss how the article should be written, and what material should be included or not.

While involved in a discussion, there are arguments that can make or break a case. Inclusion of material can hinge on what existing [policies and guidelines](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:PG "wikilink") allow for. Therefore, all arguments in such a discussion should be based on these, and not one's personal perceptions.

Unlike a [deletion discussion](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:AFD "wikilink"), where people "vote" with terms like "keep," "delete," and a variety of other actions, talk page discussions are not as formal and can follow any number of structures. The examples below use terms like "include" and "remove" just to get the point across. But a real discussion may appear quite differently.

## 근거 없는 주장

### 투표성 의견

*Examples:*

  - **Include** – <span style="color:inherit;">ThoughtlessMcInclude</span> 01:01, 1 January 2001 (UTC)
  - **Remove** – <span style="color:inherit;">ThoughtlessMcRemove</span> 01:01, 1 January 2001 (UTC)
  - **Include** because it should be included – <span style="color:inherit;">Because This</span> 01:01, 1 January 2001 (UTC)
  - **Remove** because it does not belong – <span style="color:inherit;">Because That</span> 01:01, 1 January 2001 (UTC)

It is important when engaging in a discussion that your comments are not [votes](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:VOTE "wikilink"). It helps in reaching a consensus when each person who comments gives a reason why they believe in their decision.

### 여론에 동의

*Examples:*

  - **Include** per consensus – <span style="color:inherit;">Echo</span> 01:01, 1 January 2001 (UTC)
  - **Remove** since that is what everyone else wants – <span style="color:inherit;">Copycat</span> 01:01, 1 January 2001 (UTC)

Discussions are a place where people present ideas in working toward a solution. Simply going along with the crowd does not present any new ideas. If you agree with one or more other users, you should specify why you think their ideas are good.

### 출처가 어디엔가 있음

*Examples:*

  - **Include** – This is obviously notable, so it could be referenced. <span style="color:inherit;">Prejudger</span> 01:01, 1 January 2001 (UTC)
  - **Include** – There must be plenty of sources. <span style="color:inherit;">Presumer</span> 01:01, 1 January 2001 (UTC)
  - **Include** – You should find sources, instead of deleting it. <span style="color:inherit;">ItsUpToYou</span> 01:01, 1 January 2001 (UTC)

We keep content because we know it can be sourced, not because we assume it can, without having seen them. Any claim that sources exist must be [verifiable](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability "wikilink"), and unless you can indicate what and where the sources are, they are not verifiable.

### 단순히 정책 또는 지침 가리키기

*Examples:*

  - **Include** Meets [WP:NOR](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:NOR "wikilink") – <span style="color:inherit;">Policylover</span>, 01:01, 1 January 2001 (UTC)
  - **Delete** per [WP:V](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:V "wikilink"), [WP:RS](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:RS "wikilink"), [WP:OR](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:OR "wikilink"), [WP:NPOV](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:NPOV "wikilink"), etc. – <span style="color:inherit;">Pilingiton</span>, 01:01, 1 January 2001 (UTC)
  - **Include** because [we should ignore all rules\!](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:IAR "wikilink") – <span style="color:inherit;">Anarwikist</span>, 01:41, 2 August 2008 (UTC)

While merely citing a policy or guideline may give other editors a clue as to what the reasoning is, it does not explain specifically how the policy applies to the discussion at hand. When asserting that content should be removed, it is important to explain why. The same is true when asserting that something does follow policy.

As noted [above](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/#Just_a_vote "wikilink"), content discussions are not "votes". They are *discussions* with the goal of determining [consensus](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:consensus "wikilink"). Rather than merely writing "[Original research](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Original_research "wikilink")", or "Does not meet [Wikipedia:Verifiability](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability "wikilink")", consider writing a more detailed summary, e.g. "[Original research](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:OR "wikilink"): Contains speculation not attributed to any sources" or "Does not meet [Wikipedia:Verifiability](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability "wikilink") – only sources cited are blogs and chat forum posts". Providing specific reasons why the subject may be original research or improperly sourced gives other editors an opportunity to supply sources that better underpin the claims made in the article.

Keep in mind that content can often be improved, and may not need to be deleted if the specific problems can be identified and corrected (see [surmountable problems](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/#Surmountable_problems "wikilink"), below.)

Also, while citing essays that summarize a position can be useful shorthand, citing an essay (like this one) just by one of its many shortcuts (e.g. [WP:ILIKEIT](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:ILIKEIT "wikilink") or [WP:IDONTLIKEIT](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:IDONTLIKEIT "wikilink")), without further explanation, is similarly ill-advised, for the reasons explained above.

### 자비 구하기

*Examples:*

  - **Include** I worked so hard on this article. Do you really want to put my contributions to waste? – <span style="color:inherit;">DoNotHurtMe</span>, 01:01, 1 January 2001 (UTC)
  - **Include** You would be doing me a big favor if you changed your opinion – <span style="color:inherit;">Mindchanger</span>, 01:01, 1 January 2001 (UTC)
  - **Include** I need more time to work on it – <span style="color:inherit;">Not Finished Yet</span>, 01:01, 1 January 2001 (UTC)
  - **Include** I am on vacation now, and I won't be able to work on it until I get back home – <span style="color:inherit;">InTahiti</span>, 01:01, 1 January 2001 (UTC)
  - **Include** I placed this template on top of the page so my work wouldn't get deleted – <span style="color:inherit;">ConstructionSign</span>, 01:01, 1 January 2001 (UTC)
  - **Include** I placed [hidden text](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:HIDDEN "wikilink") next to my work telling others they were not supposed to delete it – <span style="color:inherit;">WarningMarker</span>, 01:01, 1 January 2001 (UTC)

Such arguments make no use of policy or guidelines whatsoever. They are merely a campaign on the part of the commentator to alter others' points-of-view. They are of no help in reaching a consensus, and anyone responding to such pleas is not helping either.

You should also make yourself familiar with [Wikipedia's canvassing guidelines](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:CANVASS "wikilink") before you solicit "votes" one way or the other in a discussion.

If you feel you need more time to work on new content, an option may be to request [userfication](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:USERFY "wikilink"), where you can spend as much time as you wish to improve the content until it meets Wikipedia's inclusion guidelines. Once this has been accomplished, you can reintroduce it into main article space.

## 개인적 관점

### 문서의 모습

  - **Include** Makes article look good – <span style="color:inherit;">Vanity Fair</span> 01:01, 1 January 2001 (UTC)
  - **Remove** Makes article look bad – <span style="color:inherit;">Eyesore Buster</span> 01:01, 1 January 2001 (UTC)

Content on Wikipedia is judged based on its [compliance with guidelines](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:PG "wikilink"), not its physical appearance. Once you can make all the content comply, you can then work with that and tidy it up.

### 개인적 취향

  - **Include** I like it in there – <span style="color:inherit;">All for me</span> 01:01, 1 January 2001 (UTC)
  - **Remove** I hate it in there – <span style="color:inherit;">Yuck</span> 01:01, 1 January 2001 (UTC)
  - **Include** This is great, and I think it should stay. – <span style="color:inherit;">Peacock</span>, 02:02, 2 February 2002 (UTC)
  - **Remove**: No need. – <span style="color:inherit;">WhoNeedsThis</span>, 06:07, 5 April 2004 (UTC)
  - **Remove**: I'm so ashamed this article is on Wikipedia. – <span style="color:inherit;">Mortified_Molly</span>, 01:31, 16 February 2010 (UTC)
  - **Remove**: Got bored of reading. Not of interest to English-speakers. – <span style="color:inherit;">HastyHannigan</span>, 03:07, 25 July 2011 (UTC)
  - **Remove**: This makes me look stupid\! – <span style="color:inherit;">Reputation Defender</span>, 19:10, 25 February 2016 (UTC)
  - **Remove** This offends me. – <span style="color:inherit;">OnTheDefense</span>, 11:47, 1 February 2015 (UTC)
  - **Remove** Does not belong here. – <span style="color:inherit;">MembersOnly</span>, 16:25, 5 February 2007 (UTC)
  - **Remove** That would never appear in a paper encyclopedia. – <span style="color:inherit;">WebExclusive</span>, 12:01, 18 December 2006 (UTC)

Such arguments are purely personal point-of-view. They make no use of policies, guidelines, or even logic. The message behind any of these is that "[I don't like it](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:IDONTLIKEIT "wikilink"), therefore it should not be included". On Wikipedia, inclusion of content is determined by a series of policies and guidelines set by consensus, not by people saying "I think this belongs" or "I do not think this belongs". All of that is personal opinion, and the only comment less helpful than personal opinion is a simple vote. It is impossible to please everyone. But it is possible to comply with guidelines, and this will decide what is included and what not. Wikipedia editors are a pretty diverse group of individuals, and potentially any subject or topic may be liked or disliked by some editor somewhere. However, personal preference is not a valid reason to keep or delete an article or other content.

As stated at [Wikipedia:Verifiability](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability "wikilink"):

> In Wikipedia, verifiability means that anyone using the encyclopedia can check that the information comes from a reliable source. Wikipedia does not publish original research. Its content is determined by previously published information rather than the beliefs or experiences of its editors. Even if you're sure something is true, it must be verifiable before you can add it.

In other words, a person or group may well be the greatest example of what they do in the history of everything, but if no other [verifiable](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:V "wikilink") [reliable sources](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:RS "wikilink") have been written about them that are relevant to the scope of the article, they cannot be included. If your favourite song/computer game/webcomic/whatever is as great as you believe, someone will likely write about it eventually, so please just be patient. On the other hand, claims that content should be excluded require an explanation of *which* policy the content fails and explanation of *why* that policy applies as the rationale for removing it.

In general, the scope and purpose of the article must be kept in mind when considering inclusion or exclusion of information or sources. When sources significantly deviate from the scope of an article's topic, or subject, this may create room for disputes. Therefore, careful considerations such as weight and relevance should also be taken into account in making decisions.

### 흥미로움

  - **Include** Makes the article more interesting – <span style="color:inherit;">WOW</span> 01:01, 1 January 2001 (UTC)
  - **Remove** No one is interested in reading that – <span style="color:inherit;">Boring\!</span> 01:01, 1 January 2001 (UTC)

There are many interesting facts out there. There are also lots of things you may perceive no one is interested in reading. You never know. Once again, this is opinion.

### 간단한 사실

  - **Include** Is verifiable – <span style="color:inherit;">C4Urself</span> 01:01, 1 January 2001 (UTC)
  - **Include** Is sourced – <span style="color:inherit;">Good Enough</span> 01:01, 1 January 2001 (UTC)

[Wikipedia is not an indiscriminate collection of information](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:INDISCRIMINATE "wikilink"). There is actually quite a lot of true and sourceable information out in this world that does not belong on Wikipedia, or in a particular article. For example, Wikipedia is not a collection of [statistics](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:NOTSTATS "wikilink"), [trivia](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:TRIVIA "wikilink"), or [how to information](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:NOTHOWTO "wikilink"). And besides, you would not write facts about an [apple tree](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/apple_tree "wikilink") in an article about [shark](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/shark "wikilink")s. Simply saying something is "verifiable" or "sourced" does not show how it is relevant to the subject or why it otherwise belongs.

### 도움이 됨

*Example:*

  - **Include** Useful. – <span style="color:inherit;">Usefulisgood</span>, 05:05, 5 May 2005 (UTC)
  - **Remove**: We don't need this here. – <span style="color:inherit;">Judgmental</span>, 03:03, 3 March 2003 (UTC)

Wikipedia is an encyclopedia, so it should include useful encyclopedic content. But many useful things do not belong in an encyclopedia and are excluded. Just saying something is useful or useless without providing explanation and context is not helpful or persuasive in the discussion. You need to say *why* content is useful or useless; this way other editors can judge whether it's useful *and* encyclopedic, and whether it meets Wikipedia's policies.

A list of all the phone numbers in [New York](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_York "wikilink") would be useful, but is not included because [Wikipedia is not a directory](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:What_Wikipedia_is_not#Wikipedia_is_not_a_directory "wikilink"). A page simply defining the word *useful* would be useful, but is not included because [Wikipedia is not a dictionary](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Wikipedia_is_not_a_dictionary "wikilink") (we have [Wiktionary](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wiktionary "wikilink") for that). A guide to the best restaurants in [Paris](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paris "wikilink") would be useful, but is not included because [Wikipedia is not a travel guide](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:What_Wikipedia_is_not#Wikipedia_is_not_an_indiscriminate_collection_of_information "wikilink") (there is a [Wikivoyage](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikivoyage "wikilink") for that). Usefulness is a subjective judgment and should be avoided unless it supports a cogent argument.

If reasons are given, "usefulness" can be the basis of a valid argument for inclusion. An encyclopedia should, by definition, be informative and useful to its readers. Try to exercise common sense, and consider how a non-trivial number of people will consider the information "useful". Information found in [tables](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:When_to_use_tables "wikilink") in particular is focused on usefulness to the reader. An argument based on usefulness can be valid if put in context. For example, "This list brings together related topics in X and is useful for navigating that subject."

There are some pages within Wikipedia that are supposed to be useful navigation tools and nothing more—[disambiguation](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:DISAMBIG "wikilink") pages, [categories](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:Categorization "wikilink"), and [redirects](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:R "wikilink"), for instance—so usefulness is the basis of their inclusion; for these types of pages, usefulness is a valid argument.

### 아무도 피해를 입지 않음

*Examples:*

  - **Include** Why change this, it is not harming anyone. – <span style="color:inherit;">Hippocrates2</span>, 05:05, 6 June 2006 (UTC)
  - **Remove** This content is very harmful to many people. Get rid of this now\! – <span style="color:inherit;">BiographyPolice</span>, 15:01, 5 September 2013 (UTC)

[No content on Wikipedia is censored](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:NOTCENSORED "wikilink"). Just because content does not directly hurt anyone does not mean it should stay in an article. For example, if there has not been any [verifiable](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:V "wikilink") information published in [reliable sources](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:RS "wikilink") about the subject, then there is no way to check whether the information is true, and it may damage the reputation of the subject and the project. Even if it *is* true, without the ability to check it, false information could very well start to seep in. As for content that does not conform to [our basic tenets](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:Principles "wikilink") ([verifiability](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:V "wikilink"), [notability](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:N "wikilink"), and using [reliable sources](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:RS "wikilink")), keeping it actually can do more harm than one realizes – it sets a precedent that dictates that literally anything can go here.

The "it does not do any harm" claim and its rebuttal are at the center of the philosophical editing debate of inclusionism versus deletionism. For more information and arguments, see the Meta articles [Inclusionism](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/:m:Inclusionism "wikilink") and [Deletionism](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/:m:Deletionism "wikilink").

In some cases, such as [WP:BLP](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:BLP "wikilink"), whether content is harmful is often a relevant issue. The rules provide that inherently disruptive pages, for instance, may be deleted. The argument "it's not hurting anything" is less persuasive, however, when [WP:NOT](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:NOT "wikilink") clearly prohibits the content in question (e.g. a full-fledged blog in userspace) from being hosted here.

### 가치있음

*Examples:*

  - **Include** valuable. – <span style="color:inherit;">Gollum</span>, 05:05, 16 Demember 2012 (UTC)
  - **Remove** adds nothing of value – <span style="color:inherit;">Scrooge</span>, 05:05, 16 Demember 2012 (UTC)
  - **Include**: This was not an advertisement, but VALUABLE INFORMATION about our groundbreaking product that everyone on the Internet seeks on Wikipedia\! – <span style="color:inherit;">I. Wanda Publicize-Sumthin</span>,
  - **Include**: This content is for a really good cause...it is about a charitable group that is trying to save children – <span style="color:inherit;">SaveTheKids\!Please\!</span>,
  - **Remove**: The government of Utopistan notes that military information on this article helps insurgents to plan attacks. – <span style="color:inherit;">SaveTheTroops\!Please\!</span>,

Value is subjective. Simply saying it has value or no value with out substantiating the position of why or how is not a helpful or persuasive contribution to a discussion. Remember, **you need to say why** the content is or is not valuable; this way other editors can judge its value in a certain context, and whether it meets Wikipedia's policies. Without that explanation, it does not make a valid argument.

Additionally, Wikipedia is [not the place](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:RGW "wikilink") to seek publicity for a cause, product, individual, ideology, etc. Promotional or partisan "information" in particular generally fails Wikipedia's requirements of [neutrality](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/:WP:NPOV "wikilink") and [verifiability](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/:WP:V "wikilink"). See also [WP:NOBLECAUSE](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:NOBLECAUSE "wikilink") and ["It's useful"](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:USEFUL "wikilink").

## 극복 가능한 문제

### 글쓰기의 질

  - **Remove** Has a lot of misspelling and poor grammar. – <span style="color:inherit;">English Teacher</span> 01:01, 1 January 2001 (UTC)
  - **Remove** This paragraph is messy and poorly laid out. – <span style="color:inherit;">LostWillToFix</span>,
  - **Remove** It's not referenced properly – <span style="color:inherit;">Lazy1</span>, 01:01, 1 January 2001 (UTC)
  - **Include** We'll find some sources later – <span style="color:inherit;">NotRightNow</span>, 01:01, 1 January 2001 (UTC)

Material that is included on Wikipedia is judged on its accuracy and strength of sourcing, not on the quality of writing. A paragraph or two that has spelling, grammar, or punctuation problems can always be corrected. Consider that [Wikipedia is a work in progress](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:WIP "wikilink") and content should not be deleted just because no one has felt like cleaning it up *yet*. Remember, [Wikipedia has no deadline](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:There_is_no_deadline "wikilink"). If there's good, eventually sourceable, content in the article, it should be developed and improved, not removed. (If there is *no* usable content, however, it may well be best to remove it.)

In the Wiki model, content which may *currently* be poorly written, poorly formatted, lack sufficient sources, or not be a comprehensive overview of the subject, can be improved and rewritten to fix its current flaws. That such content is lacking in certain areas is a relatively minor problem, and it can still be of benefit to Wikipedia. In other words, the remedy for such content is cleanup, *not* removal.

With that said, if content is so bad that it is *harmful* in its current state, then removing it now, and possibly adding it back later, is often a better option. For example, problems like [copyright infringement](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:COPYRIGHT "wikilink"), [advertising](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:SPAM "wikilink"), [patent nonsense](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:NONSENSE "wikilink"), or unsourced negative statements in [biographies of living people](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:LIVING "wikilink"), need to be resolved as quickly as possible. Additionally, if specific problems with content have been known for some time, such as when statements are tagged with a {{citation needed}} template, that *may* be grounds for concluding that no sources actually exist.

### 접근 불가능한 출처

  - **Remove** The only sources given are offline. – <span style="color:inherit;">Cantmakeittothelibrary</span>, 03:29, 22 August 2007 (UTC)
  - **Remove** With only offline sources given, there is no [proof](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:PROVEIT "wikilink") that this is not a hoax. – <span style="color:inherit;">The Secret Keeper</span>, 03:29, 22 August 2007 (UTC)
  - **Remove** One look online shows that this does not even exist. – <span style="color:inherit;">Jumping to conclusions</span>, 03:29, 22 August 2007 (UTC)
  - **Remove** Google Books only allows you to see pages 1-45 of this book, and the source claims it's on page 57. – <span style="color:inherit;">Restricted access</span>, 03:29, 22 August 2007 (UTC)
  - **Remove** All the sources given have fallen victim to [linkrot](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:LINKROT "wikilink"). Therefore, we have no way of knowing about this. – <span style="color:inherit;">Evidence Destroyed</span>, 03:29, 22 August 2007 (UTC)
  - **Remove** The book sources don't have any [ISBN](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/ISBN "wikilink")s, so they must be fake. <span style="color:inherit;">IAmANumber</span>, 03:29, 22 August 2007 (UTC)
  - **Remove** The sources are behind a paywall. <span style="color:inherit;">InformationWantsToBeFree</span>, 03:29, 22 August 2007 (UTC)
  - **Remove** I can't access this so I don't know if it's true. <span style="color:inherit;">Suspicious</span>, 03:29, 22 August 2007 (UTC)
  - **Oppose** I won't accept your argument until you cite sources I can check myself. <span style="color:inherit;">NoTrust</span>, 03:29, 22 August 2007 (UTC)

On Wikipedia, we [assume good faith](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:AGF "wikilink"). Unless we have good reason to suspect that an editor is mistaken or dishonest, we take their contributions to discussions at face value. This doesn't mean we can't doubt what someone says, but it means that we need actual reasons to do so.

*There is no distinction between using online versus offline sources.* [Offline sources](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:OFFLINE "wikilink") are just as legitimate as those that are accessible to everyone online. If content is sourced to offline sources, even exclusively offline sources, we give the creator (and other contributors) the benefit of the doubt in accepting their accuracy. Since Wikipedia is written collaboratively, it is always possible for other editors to add online sources on top of the offline ones already there. However, this is not a requirement, and they need not exist to sustain the content. That said, all sources must be verifiably [published](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:Verifiability "wikilink") and thus accessible somehow (even if at expense and trouble), as well as [reliable](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:RS "wikilink").

### 아무도 갱신하고 있지 않음 (또는 발전에 대한 성급함)

*Examples:*

  - **Remove** This hasn't been improved in 2 years\! – <span style="color:inherit;">TheyDidntWork</span>, 03:29, 22 August 2007 (UTC)
  - **Remove** I gave them six months for someone to add cites, they didn't, and I have lost my patience. – <span style="color:inherit;">My Way or the Highway</span>, 01:33, 12 October 2010 (UTC)
  - **Remove** Creator has totally neglected this article – <span style="color:inherit;">Plant and run</span>, 03:29, 22 August 2007 (UTC)
  - **Remove** Each time we discussed this, User:WantItKept promised they would improve it. But that never happened. And User:WantItKept keeps reneging on his promise. <span style="color:inherit;">Last straw was long ago</span>, 03:29, 22 August 2007 (UTC)
  - **Include** I know I can improve it, I just have no time now to explain how. <span style="color:inherit;">PrettyPleaseWithACherryOnTop</span>, 03:29, 22 August 2007 (UTC)

Sometimes content is not being worked on very much, or has not been edited in a long time, and thus might not be in very good shape. This does not necessarily mean that the material is unsuitable for Wikipedia; it may be that the topic is obscure or difficult to write about. Content should be assessed on its own merits, not how frequently it has been edited to date. Remember that [there is no deadline](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:There_is_no_deadline "wikilink").

Content shouldn't be removed just because no one has improved it yet; that would  editors from improving it in the future. Conversely it's not enough to promise to make the content better; editors should explain how to do it. If the editor fails to follow through on the promise, other editors who arrive later can step in and keep improving it. This way, the fate of the content is not dependent on [one single editor doing the work](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:NOTMANDATORY "wikilink"); Wikipedia is [written in a collaborative way](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:WIP "wikilink").

**Exceptions:**

  - Content of a potentially dubious nature that has not been sourced properly or at all should be removed on sight from any [biographies of living people](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:Biographies_of_living_people "wikilink").
  - Material that is [unambiguous advertising or promotion](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:Spam "wikilink") may simply be deleted, if there is little to no content worth saving.
  - Other exceptions include [unambiguous copyright infringement](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:COPYVIO "wikilink") and [edits by a blocked or banned user in violation of their block or ban](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:BANREVERT "wikilink").
  - While these sorts of arguments may not be good arguments for removal of content, they often are excellent arguments for a merge of sections or pages in cases where an excessive number of undeveloped sections or redundant pages exist. (A counter-exception is that [user essays](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:Essay#User_essays "wikilink") are usually left as-is.)

## Relevance and significance fallacies

### Sourcing

  - **Include** Despite the fact there is no sourcing, I know this is accurate – <span style="color:inherit;">KnowsBest</span> 01:01, 1 January 2001 (UTC)

On Wikipedia, inclusion is based on [verifiability, not truth](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:V "wikilink"). All included information must be verifiable by [reliable sources](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:RS "wikilink").

### Personal knowledge

  - **Include** I am an expert on this subject – <span style="color:inherit;">TheUltimateAuthority</span> 01:01, 1 January 2001 (UTC)
  - **Include** I work for this company and know this is about to happen there – <span style="color:inherit;">Insider</span> 01:01, 1 January 2001 (UTC)

[No original research](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:OR "wikilink") is allowed on Wikipedia. Word-of-mouth info that is not from published sources is not considered to be verifiable.

### Unreliable sources

  - **Include** Someone on this blog said that it is true – <span style="color:inherit;">OneIsEnough</span> 01:01, 1 January 2001 (UTC)
  - **Include** A friend told me about it on Facebook – <span style="color:inherit;">Word of mouth</span> 01:01, 1 January 2001 (UTC)
  - **Include** There is a whole web site devoted to this subject – <span style="color:inherit;">Make It Notable Yourself</span>, 13:13, 08 June 2007 (UTC)
  - **Include** Look what I found about it on Twitter – <span style="color:inherit;">Tweet Me</span>, 13:13, 08 June 2007 (UTC)

[Verifiability](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:V "wikilink") requires that content must be sourced to [reliable sources](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:RS "wikilink"). Sites like [blog](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/blog "wikilink")s and personal pages that can be created or edited by anyone with little or no restriction are generally not seen as reliable sources of information. While such sites may be written in good faith and may be seen by some as accurate and/or neutral, there is little or no control or proof of these details, and there is even a chance they may have been created or edited by the very same person who created or contributed to the Wikipedia article.

On the other hand, blogs can be written by professional journalists and subject to editorial control, and personal sites can belong to established experts in the subject. There are also pages bearing the URLs of blogs that have mirrored news articles that do constitute reliable sources. For sites including user-generated content, assess whether the content is self-published or [can be attributed](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:RSOPINION "wikilink") to an independent professional writer with a record of reliable publishing. News sources that publish in a blog format may be as reliable as a traditional newspaper.

### Trivial information

  - **Include** Without this information and the sources that come along with it, the article will be completely unreferenced, and will be in danger of deletion. – <span style="color:inherit;">Anchor Baby</span> 01:01, 1 January 2001 (UTC)
  - **Include** In all the publicity this school has received, they mention this particular honors student – <span style="color:inherit;">WayToGo\!</span>, 13:13, 08 June 2007 (UTC)
  - **Include** This was mentioned in the community times – <span style="color:inherit;">HighRatings</span>, 13:13, 08 June 2007 (UTC)
  - **Include** I heard someone say on the 5 o'clock news that the police have been called to this gas station – <span style="color:inherit;">NuisanceProperty</span>, 13:13, 08 June 2007 (UTC)
  - **Remove** One sentence is trivial coverage. <span style="color:inherit;">HennyYoungman</span>, 13:13, 08 June 2007 (UTC)

In order to meet the [general notability guideline](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:GNG "wikilink"), an article must be sourced by reliable sources with *substantial coverage*. If only the trivia in an article is and can be sourced, it is doubtful whether or not it can be included, and this should indeed be examined.

The subject doesn't have to be the main topic of the source material, as long as we have enough sources to write from a [neutral point of view](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:NPOV "wikilink"). Critical commentary from [reputable](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:RS "wikilink") [professional reviewers](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:PROFESSIONAL "wikilink") and [prestigious awards](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:ORG#cite_note-2 "wikilink") are examples of short but *significant* (i.e. nontrivial) mentions that have been used to establish notability and are useful to write *Reception* sections (see the specific guidelines for [books](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Notability_\(books\)#Criteria "wikilink"), [films](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Notability_\(films\) "wikilink"), [music](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Notability_\(music\) "wikilink") and [artists](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:AUTHOR "wikilink")); common sense and editorial judgement should be used to reach a consensus about the sources available.

### Unchallenged material (or content age)

  - **Include**: Has remained in the article for 6 years already and no one has challenged it. – <span style="color:inherit;">Dried up</span> 01:01, 1 January 2001 (UTC)
  - **Oppose change**: This page has said this since the wikiproject was started\! – \<span style"color:inherit;"\>SovereignProject</span> 22:19, 15 March 2016 (UTC)
  - **Keep**: I inserted that two months ago and no one objected then, thus is must have consensus. – \<span style"color:inherit;"\>UnclearOnTheConcept</span> 14:43, 15 March 2016 (UTC)

The argument also exists in inverted form:

  - **Remove**: This has only been here for two days, and the page was stable before that. Why should we keep this addition? – \<span style"color:inherit;"\>StatusQuoStonewall Jackson</span> 02:12, 15 March 2016 (UTC)

On Wikipedia, [nothing is set in stone](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:STONE "wikilink"). Any text on any page is subject to change at any time, no matter how long it has been that way. If there is a good reason to remove long-standing text, the length of time it has been there should not be an obstacle.

Just as [inclusion is not an indicator of notability](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:Inclusion_is_not_an_indicator_of_notability "wikilink"), it is also not an indicator of significance or relevance. Bits of outright hoax material have survived in articles sometimes for years, and various proclamations of would-be "guidelines" at [wikiproject advice pages](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:PROJPAGE "wikilink") have contradicted actual policy for a long time without being corrected. While [WP:Consensus](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:Consensus "wikilink") policy reminds us that any undiscussed edit that is not disputed by later [can be {{em](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Silence_and_consensus "wikilink"), the act of challenging it (in good faith) removes that default assumption, by definition. "It's been here a long time" does not equate to "it has had actual consensus for a long time". The material might have achieved its age because its lack of relevance, significance, or current applicability was not discovered until recently.

There is a big difference between material that, on the one hand, someone simply inserted and no one bothered to talk about until now, and, on the other, material that has been repeatedly challenged and retained (by source- and/or policy-based consensus, not a [false consensus](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:FALSECONSENSUS "wikilink")). Outside mainspace, material that has become integral to how Wikipedia operates is also not subject to sudden removal or radical alteration simply because someone isn't clear where consensus established it back when. However, no amount of longevity of some item in an article or other page [is sufficient](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:LOCALCONSENSUS "wikilink") to surmount a conflict with current [Wikipedia policies and guidelines](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:Policies_and_guidelines "wikilink"), which may have changed since the material was inserted. [Consensus can change](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:CCC "wikilink"), and a standard operating procedure on Wikipedia is the normalization of old material to incremental changes in policy.

There is also a difference between A) resisting poorly-thought-out changes that did not have solid rationales, and B) [status-quo stonewalling](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:STONEWALL "wikilink") to [exert control over](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:OWN "wikilink") a page, even if proposed changes are well-justified. As a matter of [the Editing policy](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:Editing "wikilink"), it is not necessary for any editor to seek "permission" before making good-faith changes. That said, being a new addition that might be improved upon does not somehow "protect" a change from being challenged [or reverted pending further discussion](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:BRD "wikilink").

In short, if some material is not suitable for Wikipedia by current standards, it will be deleted or corrected, regardless of how old it is.

### Google test

*Examples:*

  - **Include** There are 345,400 Google hits for this term, so it is clearly of interest. – <span style="color:inherit;">GoogleBoy</span>, 04:04, 4 April 2004 (UTC)
  - **Remove** Only 10 Google hits, non-notable. – <span style="color:inherit;">GoogleGirl</span>, 04:04, 4 April 2004 (UTC)
  - **Remove** Zero Google hits, must be a hoax. – <span style="color:inherit;">MustBeAHoax</span>, 04:04, 4 April 2004 (UTC)
  - **Include** She's the first Google result for her name, so obviously she's important. – <span style="color:inherit;">FirstIsBest</span>, 04:04, 4 April 2004 (UTC)

Although using a search engine like [Google](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/Google "wikilink") can be useful in determining how common or well-known a particular topic is, a large number of hits on a search engine is no guarantee that the subject is suitable for inclusion in Wikipedia. The *quality* of the search engine results matters more than the raw number. A more detailed description of the problems that can be encountered using a search engine to determine suitability can be found at [Wikipedia:Search engine test](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Search_engine_test "wikilink").

Similarly, a lack of search engine hits may only indicate that the topic is highly specialized or not generally sourceable via the internet. [WP:BIO](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:BIO "wikilink"), for instance, specifically states, *Avoid criteria based on search engine statistics (e.g., Google hits or Alexa ranking)*. One would not expect to find thousands of hits on an [ancient Estonian god](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vanatühi "wikilink"). However, the search-engine test *may* be useful as a negative test of popular culture topics which one would expect to see sourced via the Internet. A search on an alleged "Internet meme" that returns only one or two distinct sources is a reasonable indication that the topic is not as important as has been claimed. As well, numerous hits that refer to X as "Y" can demonstrate that "Y" is a plausible redirect to the article on X.

Note further that searches using Google's specialty tools, such as [Google Books](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/Google_Books "wikilink"), [Google Scholar](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/Google_Scholar "wikilink"), and [Google News](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/Google_News "wikilink") are more likely to return [reliable sources](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:RS "wikilink") that can be useful in improving content than the default Google *web* search. However, since content can be verified entirely by [offline sources](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:OFFLINE "wikilink") such as books and newspapers, a lack of search results there is not proof in itself that content should be kept or deleted.

### Number of editors involved

*Examples:*

  - **Include** A lot of editors have worked on this – <span style="color:inherit;">Busy at work</span>, 13:37, 1 May 2008 (UTC)
  - **Remove** This content was written by a single editor – <span style="color:inherit;">My Personal Article</span>, 13:37, 1 May 2008 (UTC)
  - **Remove** The only editor who ever worked on this has left Wikipedia – <span style="color:inherit;">Who Cares?</span>, 13:37, 1 May 2008 (UTC)

The number of editors involved may point out the level of interest in a subject, but it does not measure the number of reliable sources or its [compliance with other policies and guidelines](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:POLICY "wikilink"). An article can be made into a good article, either by one person or by a dozen. And [if no reliable sources exist at all, then no matter how many editors they are, they will not be found](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:AMOUNT "wikilink").

### Article size

*Examples:*

  - **Include** I've added a lot of information on the subject – <span style="color:inherit;">Book of Wealth</span>, 13:13, 08 June 2007 (UTC)
  - **Remove** Article is only one line. Clearly a DICDEF – <span style="color:inherit;">Mr. Webster</span>, 13:13, 08 June 2007 (UTC)
  - **Include** We don't have enough information about this – <span style="color:inherit;">Flashcard</span>, 13:13, 08 June 2007 (UTC)
  - **Include** There's so much information here that some of it has to be good – <span style="color:inherit;">Impressed by Length</span>, 13:13, 08 June 2007 (UTC)
  - **Remove** because it's [WP:TLDR](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:TLDR "wikilink") – <span style="color:inherit;">DidntRead</span>, 13:13, 08 June 2007 (UTC)

[Wikipedia is not a collection of indiscriminate information](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:INDISCRIMINATE "wikilink"). An article could have many paragraphs or even pages of information. If any of that information is not and cannot be properly sourced, it does not belong. On the other hand, even a small amount of information can be eligible for inclusion, provided that the inclusion guidelines are met. Even if the article on a subject is very short, it may just be waiting for expansion. Likewise, information being too detailed may be a good argument for moving it to a more specialized article, but is not in itself a reason to remove it entirely.

### Subjective importance

*Examples:*

  - **Remove** Well I've never heard of it so it must be a hoax. – <span style="color:inherit;">Iknownothing</span>, 00:07, 1 April 2004 (UTC)
  - **Remove** People in my city haven't heard of this, so it must be unimportant. – <span style="color:inherit;">Provincial</span>, 15:55, 24 January 2007 (UTC)
  - **Remove** Who outside of (name locality) has ever even heard of this person/place/thing? – <span style="color:inherit;">Notknownhere</span>, 14:12, 18 June 2007 (UTC)
  - **Include** I know it well. It's on my way to school. – <span style="color:inherit;">Myneighborhood</span>, 14:12, 18 June 2007 (UTC)
  - **Include** John is the tallest person in my home town so he should be on the list of the world's tallest people. – <span style="color:inherit;">Smalltownboy</span>, 05:05, 5 May 2005 (UTC)
  - **Include** Is the only elementary school on Clubbington Street in Eastgrove. – <span style="color:inherit;">OnlySchool</span>, 07:57, 30 July 2008 (UTC)

Lots of things are well-known to a select group of people. A woman may be considered the greatest [crochet](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/crochet "wikilink")er in a local crochet group, which may make her famous in that community, but that isn't necessarily significant enough to include in a Wikipedia article. Wikipedia [is not an indiscriminate collection of information](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:IINFO "wikilink"), meaning that some things are not suitable for inclusion in Wikipedia. Everything in Wikipedia needs to be [verifiable](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:V "wikilink") information published in [reliable sources](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:RS "wikilink") before content can even be considered for inclusion, otherwise it could be considered [original research](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:NOR "wikilink"). If the only sources that have written about a subject are those within a small community it's likely (*but not always the case*) that those sources are not reliable enough to warrant inclusion in Wikipedia.

Arguments that state a subject is unknown or not well known among English readers encourage a [systemic bias](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/systemic_bias "wikilink") on Wikipedia. To avoid this, Wikipedia should include *all* significant information, even if it isn't significant within the English-speaking population or within more populous or Internet-connected nations. Likewise, arguments that state that because information is lesser known or even completely unknown outside a given locality do not mean the information cannot be included.

### Crystal ball

*Examples:*

  - **Include** This information may be unknown now, but it is going to be really important very soon. – <span style="color:inherit;">Youwillsee</span>, 18:49, 13 June 2007 (UTC)
  - **Include** Of course this unreleased single should be included. It's by The Scrotums. – <span style="color:inherit;">Mycrystalballisinforservice</span>, 01:40, 10 March 2010 (UTC)
  - **Include** We all know there will be a presidential election in 2032. – <span style="color:inherit;">Everyone's a psychic</span>, 01:40, 10 March 2010 (UTC)
  - **Remove** This celeb is just a flash in the pan, and nobody will remember her in a week/month/year. – <span style="color:inherit;">Shortattentionspan</span>, 18:49, 13 June 2007 (UTC)

Wikipedia is [not a crystal ball](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:CRYSTAL "wikilink"), and editors should avoid using one when commenting. It is difficult to determine precisely what people believe in the present, even more difficult to predict how perceptions will change in the future, and completely unnecessary to even try. Inclusion is based on objective evidence of whether sufficient reliable sources have *already* taken notice, not on subjective judgments of whether people *will* or *should* take notice in the future. Focusing on the objective evidence helps the discussion reach a logical conclusion; injecting your personal predictions does not.

### Inaction by sources

*Examples:*

  - **Remove** None of the source coverage would have occurred had the one event not occurred. – <span style="color:inherit;">Lookherenotthere</span>, 10:28, 13 April 2008 (UTC)
  - **Remove** We don't have any sources that mention this after 1990. - <span style="color:inherit;">Lookherenotthere</span>, 14:32, 13 April 2008 (UTC)

Inclusion of content is based on objective evidence of whether sufficient reliable sources  exist, not on subjective judgments of why there aren't more or newer sources. Focusing on the objective evidence helps the discussion reach a logical conclusion; injecting your personal supposition does not.

However, especially for science-related material where understanding in the field may change rapidly, old sources that are contradicted by newer, equally reliable ones, must not be given [undue weight](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:UNDUE "wikilink").

### Inaction by editors

*Examples:*

  - **Remove**: Nobody has added sources for this information, so it's not verifiable. – HopToIt, 13:01, 29 January 2010 (UTC)
  - **Reverting again** since you didn't resolve the problem I pointed out, and it doesn't really matter if others don't see it. – MyWayOrHighway, 09:32, 15 March 2016 (UTC)
  - **Remove** until you fix the problems with the rest of the article.
  - **Remove** because nobody added this to the other article on the same topic.

Verifiability is based upon whether [reliable sources](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:RS "wikilink") exist, rather than the state of sourcing in articles; [Wikipedia's Verifiability policy](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:Verifiability "wikilink") requires that information be verifi not already verifi. If the very existence of sources cannot be ascertained, this may be a valid rationale for removal. Only in [biographies of living people](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:Biographies_of_living_people "wikilink") (for [defamation](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/defamation "wikilink") legal reasons) is lack of a citation by itself grounds for deletion of material that clearly can be but has not yet been sourced.

In actual practice, many additions without sources are [reverted pending further discussion](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:BRD "wikilink") and sourcing. However, if the addition is not controversial and sources have been identified but not yet added, it may be interpreted as ["gaming the system"](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:GAMING "wikilink") to insist on its removal pending the citations, especially if the editor who added it has requested help with the sourcing (e.g. because the source is behind a journal site's paywall and they no longer have access to it).

Similar arguments are sometimes made along the lines that the objector's concerns have not been addressed yet, even though a solution to (or refutation of) them has already been provided, and there appears to be no agreement that the objector's position is correct. [Consensus](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:Consensus "wikilink") need not be unanimous to be achieved, and other editors' efforts to continue improving a page [are not](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:Editing "wikilink") held hostage by [unreasonable demands](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:FILIBUSTER "wikilink"). A call for action by one editor does not create an obligation on the part of any other to act upon it.

### Subjects are connected

*Examples:*

  - **Include** We should say that she once worked with someone famous – <span style="color:inherit;">Keeper</span>, 14:15, 03 March 2009 (UTC)
  - **Include** All examples of *foo* should be discussed in the article. – <span style="color:inherit;">Classifier</span>, 01:15, 03 January 2006 (UTC)
  - **Include**: this person's brother is famous, so he's famous too. – <span style="color:inherit;">Adrian Listmaker</span>, 18:20, 26 March 2009 (UTC)
  - **Remove** her actions shouldn't be mentioned here, she's only known because the U.S. President's wife – <span style="color:inherit;">First Lady</span> 18:16, 10 April 2015 (UTC)
  - **Include** This is an album by a famous artist, so it's relevant in this article. – <span style="color:inherit;">The internet's busiest music nerd</span>, 9:29 15 August 2015 (UTC)
  - **Include** This subject has its own article, so it should be mentioned in this article. – <span style="color:inherit;">Wheredoesitend</span>, 9:29 15 September 2015 (UTC)

Content does not qualify for inclusion merely because it exists, even if zero independent reliable sources have ever taken notice of the subject. We can't argue that "____ should be included, because it is associated with [Important Subject](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/Example "wikilink")." If subjects seem to be related, but no sources connect them to each other, making the connection in Wikipedia is [original research](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:OR "wikilink").

Sources about one or more members of some group or class of subjects may or may not apply to other possible members of that group. Discuss based upon the individual subject, not the subject's overarching classification or type. In addition, information about a parent entity or topic (of a parent-child "tree") does not *always* apply to the subordinate entities.

### Wikipedias in other languages

*Examples*

  - **Include**: given that its also in corresponding articles on other Wikipedias (de:Foo, es:Foo, fr:Foo, it:Foo, la:Foo, pt:Foo). They can't all be wrong. – <span style="color:inherit;">Interwikis=Notability</span>, 14:54, 11 August 2009 (UTC)
  - **Include**: there was a long discussion at the French Wikipedia and they decided to include it.
  - **Remove**: This material about Mexico isn't covered in the same article at the Spanish Wikipedia. – <span style="color:inherit;">TrappedBehindTheLanguageBarrier</span>, 01:15, 8 October 2009 (UTC)
  - **Remove**: This material would be against policy on the Arabic Wikipedia.
  - **Delete**: German Wikipedia doesn't have a guideline about this, so we shouldn't either.

Information will often be covered by Wikipedia articles in [many languages other than English](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_Wikipedias "wikilink"); however, this does not indicate, by itself, that it should be included here. Other Wikipedias may have different inclusion criteria from the English Wikipedia. Other versions of Wikipedia are not [reliable sources](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:RS "wikilink"), and many articles in other Wikipedias are based on translations of English Wikipedia articles. Moreover, because of the availability of online translation tools, it's easier to create cross-wiki spam. The hoax article [Jean Moufot](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:List_of_hoaxes_on_Wikipedia/Jean_Moufot "wikilink") was first posted on Netherlands Wikipedia and then translated into several other languages, including English. Of course, if the other Wikipedia articles cite any reliable sources not in the English Wikipedia article, they can be added to it.

On the other hand, the fact that corresponding articles at other Wikipedias don't have something that en.Wikipedia does is not a reason that the information should be removed. It may be the case that nobody has yet added this to the other language's Wikipedia or that it just hasn't been linked to from the English language article. It may also be the information is important in the English-speaking world, but of little relevance to speakers of other languages, or vice versa. En.Wikipedia also sees more editorial activity on most topics than other-language Wikipedia's do. Finally, all of the various Wikipedias set most of their policies and guidelines independently, aside from legal and WMF-mission-related requirements imposed on them by [WP:OFFICE](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:OFFICE "wikilink").

## 개별적 가치

### 다른 내용도 똑같지 않나요?

*Examples:*

  - **Include** This information is in article x as well. – <span style="color:inherit;">EmperorOtherstuff</span>, 04:04, 4 April 2004 (UTC)
  - **Include** That other article has information that's even less important. – <span style="color:inherit;">PokePerson:O</span>, 04:04, 4 April 2004 (UTC)
  - **Include** You say this is promotional, but there are other articles with content just as promotional as this one. – <span style="color:inherit;">Blay Tant Marqueter</span>, 04:04, 25 December 2010 (UTC)
  - **Include** If you remove this you have to remove the whole section. – <span style="color:inherit;">AllOrNothing</span>, 12:04, 3 August 2006 (UTC)
  - **Remove** Article y doesn't mention this, so article x shouldn't either. – <span style="color:inherit;">EmpressOtherstuff</span>, 04:04, 4 April 2004 (UTC)
  - **Remove** You guys forced me to remove the information at article x, so you have to allow me to remove the information at article y. – <span style="color:inherit;">NoFair</span>, 04:04, 4 April 2004 (UTC)

The nature of Wikipedia means that you cannot make a convincing argument based solely on whether or not the same or similar content exists or is formatted similarly in some other page; this is because there is nothing stopping anyone from editing or creating any article. While these comparisons are not a conclusive test, they may form part of a cogent argument; an entire comment should not be dismissed because it includes a comparative statement like this. While comparing with other articles is not, in general, a convincing argument, comparing with articles that have been through some kind of quality review such as [Featured article](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:Featured_article "wikilink"), [Good article](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:Good_article "wikilink"), or have achieved a [WikiProject A class rating](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:QUALITY "wikilink"), makes a much more credible case, if the review does not pre-date policy changes that affect the material.

When an editor introduces new information to Wikipedia, it may be necessary to consider whether the inclusion and organization of such material is compliant with core policies such as [neutral point of view](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:NPOV "wikilink") and [no original research](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:NOR "wikilink"). Other editors may argue that a certain type of information isn't included because of inherent violations of core policies; see [WP:ATTACK](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:ATTACK "wikilink") for example. Dismissing such concerns simply by pointing to this essay is inappropriate.

## Meta-reasoning

### Wikipedia should be about everything

*Examples:*

  - **Include** I thought Wikipedia's purpose was to provide information on everything. – <span style="color:inherit;">AllInclusive</span>, 12:04, 3 August 2006 (UTC)
  - **Include** You are trying to remove true information\! – <span style="color:inherit;">AllTruthful</span>, 15:45, 17 December 2006 (UTC)
  - **Include** This thing exists, so it should be included. – <span style="color:inherit;">JohnPaulSartre\&Ringo</span>, 01:14, 14 June 2007 (UTC)

Yes, Wikipedia is an encyclopedia, and as such, it should convey information on all branches of knowledge. However, "all branches of knowledge" does *not* necessarily mean "everything". Wikipedia is specifically [not an indiscriminate collection of information](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:NOT#Wikipedia_is_not_an_indiscriminate_collection_of_information "wikilink"), which means there are standards for what constitutes information that should be in Wikipedia. Imagine how large [an encyclopedia on everything would be](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/Infinite_monkey_theorem#Origins_and_"The_Total_Library" "wikilink"): everything would include every idea that has existed or will exist, every person who ever lived, every organization that has existed or exists, every copy of an object that has existed or exists, every website that has existed or exists, etc. The most basic threshold of inclusion is [verifiability, not truth](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:V "wikilink"). The verifiability requirement alone would prevent writing about every particle and limit the information that could be included on every person. Moreover, the community has decided not to document every verifiable fact and accordingly has established guidelines on what content should be kept, and a [due weight](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:DUE "wikilink") policy on what facts are [minority views](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:FRINGE "wikilink"). Even though that guideline is [broader than a paper encyclopedia](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:NOTPAPER "wikilink")'s guidelines, it is also not "everything". So think carefully and exercise judgement when determining what should be included in an encyclopedia.

### That's only a guideline, proposal or essay

  -
    *See also: [Wikipedia:Quote your own essay](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Quote_your_own_essay "wikilink")*

*Examples:*

  - **Include** [WP:EXAMPLE](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:EXAMPLE "wikilink") is an essay, not policy. – <span style="color:inherit;">DissentingView</span>, 18:49, 13 June 2007 (UTC)
  - **Remove** [WP:Notability (ABC)](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:Notability_\(ABC\) "wikilink") is only a proposal. – <span style="color:inherit;">Idontlikeproposals</span>, 18:49, 13 June 2007 (UTC)
  - **Remove** [WP:XYZ](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:XYZ "wikilink") is only a guideline. – <span style="color:inherit;">GuidelinesNoGood</span>, 18:49, 13 June 2007 (UTC)

[Wikipedia is not a system of laws](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:BURO "wikilink"). Discussions are [not votes](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Discuss,_don't_vote "wikilink"), and we encourage people to put forward their *opinions*. Sometimes, they will find an existing project page which sums up their reasoning already, and rather than [reinventing the wheel](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/reinventing_the_wheel "wikilink") they will link to it (with a [suitable explanation](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:JUSTAPOLICY "wikilink") of why it applies). If someone links to an essay, proposal or guideline, they are not suggesting "[WP:EXAMPLE](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:EXAMPLE "wikilink") says we should do this", but rather "I believe we should do this, [WP:EXAMPLE](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:EXAMPLE "wikilink") explains the reasons why".

[Essays](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/Category:Wikipedia_essays "wikilink"), in general, serve to summarize a position, opinion or argument. [Proposals](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:PROPOSAL "wikilink"), in addition to their primary function, also summarize positions, opinions and arguments. Frequently, this is done with reference to policies and guidelines, so to glibly brand them as "only an essay" or "only a proposal" may be misleading. It also essentially suggests that the opinion of the person citing the page (as well as those of the people who originally wrote the page) is invalid when it may not be. There are many reasons why some arguments presented in discussions are invalid, based around the substance of the argument or the logic employed in reaching it. "The page you linked to is an essay or proposal" is not one of them.

Guidelines do indeed have exceptions; however, it is unhelpful to suggest "[WP:EXAMPLE](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:EXAMPLE "wikilink") is only a guideline, we do not have to follow it". We have policies which tell us *what* to do and *why* to do it, and guidelines to help us with *how* to do it. Rather than using a page's "guideline" designation as an excuse to make an exception, suggest reasons why an exception should be made.

## 사람에 대해

### [인신공격의 오류](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/인신공격의_오류 "wikilink")

*Examples:*

  - **Include** The author of this has a history of writing good content. – <span style="color:inherit;">GoodCreator</span>, 11:10, 31 July 2008 (UTC)
  - **Remove** The author has made only 27 edits so far. – <span style="color:inherit;">FewEdits</span>, 11:10, 31 July 2008 (UTC)
  - **Remove** The author has previously been reverted a lot and therefore makes poor choices. – <span style="color:inherit;">BadAuthor</span>, 11:10, 31 July 2008 (UTC)
  - **Remove** Politically motivated. – <span style="color:inherit;">POVPusher</span> 00:02, 16 April 2011

A content discussion is about the content in question itself. Though the suitability of related content may be mentioned during the discussion, and some discussions are bundled together, the debate is not about the author or any other editors of the article. Content is to be judged on its own merits and not those of its editors or detractors. Even well-respected editors sometimes make edits that others feel should be reverted, and likewise, [newbies](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:NEWBIE "wikilink") and those who have created lots of poor content still have the potential to contribute good content in the future.

There is [no shame](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:NOSHAME "wikilink") in having one's [good-faith](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:AGF "wikilink") efforts opposed by the majority. Wikipedia is not a club of [winners](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:WINNER "wikilink") and losers. If a user is disrupting the encyclopedia by continually adding information that gets deleted or removing good content from articles, an investigation may be called for into their behavior; this is an independent issue and its result one way or the other should not influence content discussions. Remember, when you comment, [personal attacks](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:NPA "wikilink") and [accusations of bad faith](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:AGF "wikilink") never help.

Edits by banned or blocked users in violation of their ban or block may be reverted entirely. This criterion does not apply to edits made *before* the ban or block, or to pages of topics unrelated to the topic of the ban (unless it is a complete site ban).

### 문서 소유권 주장

*Examples:*

  - **Include** I created this article – <span style="color:inherit;">MyPage</span> 01:01, 1 January 2001 (UTC)
  - **Remove** I added this then changed my mind, so my opinion should count for more – <span style="color:inherit;">Regretful</span> 01:01, 1 January 2001 (UTC)

On Wikipedia, [pages are not owned](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:OWN "wikilink"), even if created by that user. Once created, it is open for anyone to edit, and once substantially edited by another user, cannot even be deleted at the request of the creator.

### 평판

  - **Include** Without this information, this company will lose business – <span style="color:inherit;">Advertiser</span> 01:01, 1 January 2001 (UTC)
  - **Remove** This article is about myself, and I don't want the world to know that about me – <span style="color:inherit;">Ashamed</span> 01:01, 1 January 2001 (UTC)

[Wikipedia is not advertising space](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:NOTADVERTISING "wikilink"). Content about notable commercial enterprises are permitted, but must be written with a [neutral point of view](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:NPOV "wikilink"), and are here only to describe the company, and not to promote or disparage it.

Even an article about oneself is not owned by that person. Other editors are permitted to add and modify <u>sourced</u> information within Wikipedia's guidelines. Even if the article says something negative about someone, if it comes from a reliable source, it is perfectly acceptable there (see [An article about yourself is nothing to be proud of](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:PROUD "wikilink")). Most likely if this happened, the negative information was in the news already, so the public already knows. Importantly, all such content must still meet the strict policies found in [WP:Biographies of living persons](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:Biographies_of_living_persons "wikilink").
