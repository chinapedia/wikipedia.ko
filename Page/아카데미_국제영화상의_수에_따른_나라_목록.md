### 내용주

  -
    Submitted films include disqualified entries.
    **a**: [Italy](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/Italy "wikilink")'s tally includes two Special Awards for *[Shoe-Shine](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shoeshine_\(film\) "wikilink")* (1946) and *[The Bicycle Thief](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Bicycle_Thief "wikilink")* (1948), as well as an Honorary Award shared with [France](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/France "wikilink") for *[The Walls of Malapaga](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Walls_of_Malapaga "wikilink")* (1949).
    **b**: [France](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/France "wikilink")'s tally includes a Special Award for *[Monsieur Vincent](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/Monsieur_Vincent "wikilink")* (1947) as well as two Honorary Awards: one for *[Forbidden Games](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/Forbidden_Games "wikilink")* (1952) and one shared with [Italy](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/Italy "wikilink") for *[The Walls of Malapaga](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Walls_of_Malapaga "wikilink")* (1949).
    **c**: [Japan](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/Japan "wikilink")'s tally includes three [Honorary Awards](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/Academy_Honorary_Award "wikilink").
    **d**: The [Soviet Union](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soviet_Union "wikilink"), which received all of its nominations for [Russian-language films](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/:분류:러시아의_영화_작품 "wikilink"), was nominated for the last time in [1984](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/57th_Academy_Awards "wikilink") and was [formally dissolved](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/History_of_the_Soviet_Union_\(1985–1991\)#Dissolution_of_the_USSR "wikilink") on 1991년 12월 26일. [Russia](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/Russia "wikilink"), [Georgia](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georgia_\(country\) "wikilink") and [Kazakhstan](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kazakhstan "wikilink") are the only [post-Soviet states](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/post-Soviet_states "wikilink") to have been nominated for the Best Foreign Language Film Award.
    **e**: [East Germany](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/East_Germany "wikilink") received its only nomination in [1976](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/49th_Academy_Awards "wikilink"), and [West Germany](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/West_Germany "wikilink") was nominated for the last time in [1985](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/58th_Academy_Awards "wikilink"). The two countries [formally reunited](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/German_reunification "wikilink") on 1990년 10월 3일. [Reunified Germany](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/Germany "wikilink") has received seven nominations since then, two of which have resulted in a successful win.
    **f**: [Czechoslovakia](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/Czechoslovakia "wikilink") was nominated for the first time in [1965](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/38th_Academy_Awards "wikilink") for a [Slovak-language film](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Shop_on_Main_Street "wikilink"). Its five other nominations, the last of which occurred in [1991](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/64th_Academy_Awards "wikilink"), were received for [Czech-language films](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/:분류:체코의_영화_작품 "wikilink"). The formerly Communist country [formally split](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dissolution_of_Czechoslovakia "wikilink") into the [Czech Republic](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/Czech_Republic "wikilink") and the [Slovak Republic](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/Slovak_Republic "wikilink") on 1993년 1월 1일. Since then, only the Czech Republic has been able to get nominated in the Foreign Language Film category.
    **g**: [Yugoslavia](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/Socialist_Federal_Republic_of_Yugoslavia "wikilink") was nominated for the first time in [1958](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/31st_Academy_Awards "wikilink") for an [Italian-language film](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Road_a_Year_Long "wikilink"). Its five other nominations, the last of which occurred in [1985](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/58th_Academy_Awards "wikilink"), were received for [Serbo-Croatian-language films](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/:분류:서보크라이나의_영화_작품 "wikilink"). The formerly Communist country [gradually disintegrated](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/Breakup_of_Yugoslavia "wikilink") at the beginning of the 1990s. [Macedonia](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/Republic_of_Macedonia "wikilink") and [Bosnia & Herzegovina](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bosnia_&_Herzegovina "wikilink") are the only [post-Yugoslav states](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/Breakup_of_Yugoslavia#New_states "wikilink") to have been nominated for the Best Foreign Language Film Award.
    **h**: The [United Kingdom](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Kingdom "wikilink") has a very small tally even though it has a [prolific film industry](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cinema_of_the_United_Kingdom "wikilink"). This is due to the fact that the overwhelming majority of British films are in [English](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/영어 "wikilink") and are thus ineligible for competition in the Foreign Language Film category. The only British films nominated for the Best Foreign Language Film Award have been [Welsh-language films](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/:분류:웨일스어_영화_작품 "wikilink").
    **i**: *[Paradise Now](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paradise_Now "wikilink")* (2005) was initially nominated as a submission from "Palestine" and presented as such on the [official Academy website](https://web.archive.org/web/20080703204819/http://www3.oscars.org/78academyawards/nomswins.html). However, following protests from [pro-Israeli groups in the United States](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/Israel_lobby_in_the_United_States "wikilink"), the Academy decided to designate it as a submission from the [Palestinian Authority](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/Palestinian_Authority "wikilink"), a move that was decried by the film's director [Hany Abu-Assad](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hany_Abu-Assad "wikilink"). During the [awards ceremony](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/78th_Academy_Awards "wikilink"), the film was eventually announced by presenter [Will Smith](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/Will_Smith "wikilink") as a submission from the [Palestinian Territories](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/Palestinian_Territories "wikilink").
    **j**: Although films produced inside the United States are not eligible for consideration for the Best Foreign Language Film Award, those produced in [U.S. overseas possessions](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/U.S._overseas_possessions "wikilink") are. [Puerto Rico](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/Puerto_Rico "wikilink"), an [unincorporated territory](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/unincorporated_territory "wikilink") of the United States, was thus able to receive a nomination for *[What Happened to Santiago](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/What_Happened_to_Santiago "wikilink")* (1989).\[1\] However, this rule was changed in 2011 barring Puerto Rican submissions.\[2\]
    **k**: This is not an official nomination. After nominations were announced, information came to light that showed that *[A Place in the World](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/A_Place_in_the_World_\(film\) "wikilink")* (1992) was wholly produced in [Argentina](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/Argentina "wikilink"), and had insufficient [Uruguay](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/Uruguay "wikilink")an artistic control. The film was declared ineligible and removed from the final ballot.
    **j**: Indians have been awarded Oscars on separate occasions. For example: [Bhanu Athaiya](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bhanu_Athaiya "wikilink") won India's first Oscar in [1983](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/55th_Academy_Awards "wikilink") for *[Gandhi](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gandhi_\(film\) "wikilink")*, [Satyajit Ray](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/Satyajit_Ray "wikilink") was given an [Honorary Oscar](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/Academy_Honorary_Award "wikilink") in [1992](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/64th_Academy_Awards "wikilink"), and [A. R.
